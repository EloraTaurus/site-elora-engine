# What Is Elora

Elora Taurus is an independent research engine with governance capability.

Elora combines research-first runtime observation with proposal-to-commit governance control. The system studies AI behaviour under bounded conditions, captures structured evidence, and treats inference as proposal generation rather than execution authority.

Execution authority is enforced at a deterministic commit boundary where admissibility is evaluated against captured policy, configuration, authority context, and relevant behavioural evidence.

## Core model

- Inference produces proposals.
- Commit authorizes or blocks outcomes.
- Execution occurs only after commit authorization.
- Replay reconstructs admissibility decisions without re-running models.
- Research surfaces collect structured runtime, intervention, and behavioural evidence.
- ERIS contributes bounded runtime intelligence about operational posture.
- EBLS is the in-development learning direction focused on teaching Elora how to recognize and reason about AI behaviours themselves.

## Why this matters

This model supports accountable operations:

- policy-class blocking instead of silent failure
- deterministic decision explanation
- operator triage and forensic review
- governance telemetry separated from raw runtime telemetry
- behaviour research tied back to evidence rather than intuition
- experimentation around non-neural learning methods without handing authority to model output
